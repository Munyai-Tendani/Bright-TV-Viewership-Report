## Bright-TV-Viewership-Report

- Problem Statement
  - BrightTV aims to grow its subscription base within the current financial year but lacks actionable insights into user behavior and content consumption patterns. The challenge is to analyze user profiles and viewing session data to identify trends, drivers of engagement, and opportunities to increase both consumption and subscriber growth.

- Purpose
  - The purpose of this analysis is to support the Customer Value Management (CVM) team by uncovering data-driven insights on user behavior, content performance, and engagement patterns. These insights will inform strategies to improve user retention, increase viewing activity, and attract new subscribers.

- Objectives
  - Analyze user demographics and viewing patterns to identify key trends in platform usage.
  - Determine the factors that influence content consumption (e.g., time of day, device type, content genre).
  - Identify low-consumption periods and recommend content strategies to boost engagement during those times.
  - Propose initiatives to grow the subscriber base, including acquisition and retention strategies.
  - Convert all timestamps from UTC to South African time (SAST) to ensure accurate temporal analysis.
  - Incorporate any additional relevant data or metrics to strengthen insights and recommendations.

-Summary of Results
  - Peak viewership occurs during evening hours and weekends, indicating strong leisure-time engagement.
  - Consumption is driven by content type (e.g., popular genres like drama/sports), ease of access (device usage), and user demographics.
  - Low engagement periods (e.g., weekday mornings) present opportunities for targeted content such as short-form or news-based programming.
  - Personalized recommendations and localized content significantly improve user retention and session frequency.
  - Growth opportunities include targeted marketing campaigns, referral programs, partnerships, and improved onboarding experiences for new users.
